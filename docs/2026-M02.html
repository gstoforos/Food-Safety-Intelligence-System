Intelligence Analysis

February 2026 produced 33 food-safety hazard recall incidents across the AFTS monitoring network, a -8% move below the prior month — the rolling baseline is too narrow for a Z estimate this early in the series. Salmonella spp. dominated with 16 of 33 incidents (48%). The composite severity index closed at 60.0/100 (elevated), with 31 Tier-1 critical events and 3 outbreak-associated events (no temporal clustering) on record.

Structurally, the month reads as geographically concentrated with mixed signal concentration (Source HHI 1736.0, Geographic Gini 0.42). For a Salmonella spp.-dominated month, the relevant failure modes are post-process environmental harbourage in Zone 1 of RTE lines, sanitation SOP drift, and cold-chain lapses — not thermal underprocess. The Tier-1 intensity ratio of 0.95× vs the rolling baseline indicates severity is in line.

Looking forward, operators in Salmonella spp.-relevant commodity categories should re-verify the single highest-leverage control this month: environmental monitoring swab frequency on RTE deli and dairy lines, or pasteurisation D-value validation on low-moisture commodities, whichever matches their product mix. The linear-trend projection for next month stands at 55 recalls (95% CI [0, 111]), with r²=0.68, slope 16.0/month and t=1.46 on 1 degrees of freedom. The estimate is exploratory because the series is short — only 3 monthly observation(s) — and the slope is not statistically significant at α=0.05. Documentation packages should be ready for rapid regulatory response given continued inspection intensity.

Process Authority Note: This window includes Clostridium botulinum or botulism-relevant recalls. The control review for such events is product-specific. For shelf-stable low-acid, acidified, aseptic, or commercially sterile products, scheduled-process adequacy under a qualified process authority is the central control. For refrigerated anaerobic products — peeled garlic, sous-vide, vacuum or modified-atmosphere packs, smoked or fermented fish — the operative controls are validated refrigeration discipline, water-activity, pH, salt content, oxygen barrier integrity, and shelf-life qualification, with or without a thermal step. Across global jurisdictions the regulatory architecture differs but the engineering principle converges. In the United States, FDA 21 CFR 113 (thermally processed low-acid canned foods) and 21 CFR 114 (acidified foods) require a scheduled process established by a qualified process authority. In the European Union, Regulation 852/2004 imposes general hygiene and HACCP obligations on food-business operators, and Regulation 2073/2005 fixes microbiological criteria — neither is a direct equivalent of US LACF/acidified-food filing, but both presuppose process control validated against a documented hazard analysis, with national LACF/acidified-food rules layered on top in some Member States. Canada's Safe Food for Canadians Regulations, FSA / FSS in the UK, FSANZ in Australia and New Zealand, and Japan's MHLW each impose comparable preventive-control obligations. The common engineering principle: low-acid, aseptic, and commercially sterile products require a scheduled process established by a qualified process authority and executed under documented GMP oversight. Recall events in this category most commonly trace to one of: an unfiled or outdated scheduled process; a deviation resolved without qualified review; a formulation or packaging change introduced without process re-evaluation; a temperature-abuse event in the cold chain; or seal-integrity loss in anaerobic packaging. Manufacturers producing low-acid canned, acidified, fermented, or anaerobically packaged products — including refrigerated anaerobic products — should confirm with a qualified process authority that filings, multi-hurdle controls, GMP programmes, and deviation handling remain current before any production change.

§ 09

Methodology & Sources

Definitions. A recall incident is a single regulator-published recall fiche / notice covering one identified product or product family. Where a single multi-country event is reported by two regulators (for example FSAI in Ireland plus FSA in the United Kingdom), each regulator's notice counts as one incident — the incident table accordingly shows the multi-jurisdictional footprint rather than de-duplicating to a single row. Tier-1 indicates an immediate public-health risk (Listeria, Salmonella, STEC, Campylobacter, Cronobacter, Vibrio, Hepatitis A, marine biotoxins, mycotoxins above regulatory limits, undeclared major allergens). Outbreak-associated means the regulator's notice cites confirmed human cases or epidemiologically-linked illnesses, not merely a positive product test.

Process authority. Analytical frameworks, severity rubrics, hazard classification, and the engineering interpretation of each recall are developed by the AFTS process-authority practice, drawing on in-house expertise in food process engineering, thermal processing, and regulatory compliance. Every view is grounded in validated process engineering: thermal processing (21 CFR 113/114), pasteurisation (PMO), aseptic and UHT, hold-tube and F-value lethality, and HACCP.

Statistical methods. Month-over-month Z-scores use the rolling-prior-months mean and sample standard deviation; the score is suppressed (n/a) when the baseline contains fewer than six months. The hotspot matrix uses standardised chi-square residuals against an independence-baseline expected count; cells with σ>2 are flagged as over-represented but are screening signals only (no multiple-comparison correction; small expected counts in many cells). Source concentration uses the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index on agency counts (HHI = Σ s²ᵢ × 10000, where sᵢ is each agency's share; <1500 diverse, 1500–2500 moderate, >2500 concentrated). Geographic distribution uses the Gini coefficient on country counts (0 = perfectly even, 1 = single-country regime; <0.4 even, 0.4–0.6 moderate, >0.6 uneven). Outbreak clusters are detected via a sliding 14-day window over same-pathogen outbreak events (cluster threshold: ≥3 events). The composite severity index (0–100) is a transparent two-component blend: 100 × (0.60 × Tier-1 share + 0.40 × outbreak rate), where Tier-1 share = Tier-1 incidents ÷ total incidents and outbreak rate = outbreak-flagged incidents ÷ total incidents (using the same criterion as the headline outbreaks KPI). For February 2026 this evaluates to 100 × (0.60 × 31/33 + 0.40 × 3/33) = 60.0. Buckets: ≥65 critical, ≥45 elevated, ≥25 moderate, <25 low. Predictive models are gated to activate only when data history meets the minimum size required for valid estimation; the linear-trend OLS reports a 95% CI but does not claim slope significance until n ≥ 12 monthly observations (so dof ≥ 10, where the t-critical at α = 0.05 falls below 2.23).

Reporting-system caveats. National recall-publication regimes differ in granularity. France's RappelConso publishes one fiche per identified product/lot configuration, often producing several fiches per single root-cause event; the headline French count therefore reflects a transparent, item-level reporting practice rather than a higher true incidence. EU member-state counts likewise reflect both the actual hazard signal and each authority's publication discipline. Treat country totals as a recall-publication signal, not as a direct food-safety league table.

Data & AI pipeline. The system aggregates regulatory recall notices from 70+ countries and 15+ agencies (FDA, USDA FSIS, RASFF, FSA, FSANZ, CFIA, RappelConso, BVL, AESAN, EFET and national authorities) into the accumulative Recalls sheet. AI narrative is produced against AFTS process-authority prompts and edited for publication. Figures, hazard names, and source URLs are preserved verbatim from regulator data.
