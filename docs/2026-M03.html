§ 01

Intelligence Analysis

March 2026 produced 66 pathogen-related recall incidents across the AFTS monitoring network, a 127.6% move above the prior month, a +52.8-sigma anomaly vs the prior-month baseline. Salmonella spp. dominated with 27 of 66 incidents (41%). The composite severity index closed at 32.7/100 (normal), with 35 Tier-1 critical events and 3 outbreak clusters on record. The standout hotspot was France × Salmonella, with 19 recalls against an independence-baseline expectation of 11.86 (stdres +2.07).

Structurally, the month reads as moderately uneven geographically with signal diversity consistent with broad regulatory engagement (Source HHI 1414.0, Geographic Gini 0.552). For a Salmonella spp.-dominated month, the relevant failure modes are post-process environmental harbourage in Zone 1 of RTE lines, sanitation SOP drift, and cold-chain lapses — not thermal underprocess. The Tier-1 intensity ratio of 1.01x vs the rolling baseline indicates severity is in line.

Looking forward, operators in Salmonella spp.-relevant commodity categories should re-verify the single highest-leverage control this month: environmental monitoring swab frequency on RTE deli and dairy lines, or pasteurisation D-value validation on low-moisture commodities, whichever matches their product mix. The linear-trend projection for next month stands at 79.0 recalls (95% CI [26.4, 131.6]), with r²=0.77. Documentation packages should be ready for rapid regulatory response given continued inspection intensity.

Process Authority Note: 7 incident(s) in this window carry thermal-processing or anaerobic-packaging signals. Shelf-stable low-acid, acidified, aseptic/UHT, hot-filled, and reduced-oxygen-packaged products must be reviewed to confirm the scheduled thermal process under a qualified process authority — required under FDA 21 CFR 113/114, EU Reg. 852/2004, CFIA SFCR, FSANZ Food Standards Code Ch. 3, and Japan's Food Sanitation Act. Typical compliance gaps: unfiled or outdated scheduled process, deviation resolved without qualified review, seal-integrity lapse, or formulation change not re-evaluated.

§ 09

Methodology & Sources

Process authority. Analytical frameworks, severity rubrics, pathogen classification, and the engineering interpretation of each recall are developed under the process authority of AFTS, drawing on in-house expertise in food process engineering, thermal processing, and regulatory compliance. Every view is grounded in validated process engineering: thermal processing (21 CFR 113/114), pasteurisation (PMO), aseptic and UHT, hold-tube and F-value lethality, and HACCP.

Statistical methods. Month-over-month Z-scores use the rolling-prior-months mean and sample standard deviation; the hotspot matrix uses standardised chi-square residuals (σ>2 flags over-representation vs independence); source concentration is quantified via the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index and geographic distribution via the Gini coefficient; outbreak clusters are detected via a sliding 14-day window over same-pathogen events. Predictive models are gated to activate only when data history meets the minimum size required for valid estimation.

Data & AI pipeline. The system aggregates regulatory recall notices from 70+ countries and 15+ agencies (FDA, USDA FSIS, RASFF, FSA, FSANZ, CFIA, RappelConso, BVL, AESAN, EFET and national authorities) into the accumulative Recalls sheet. AI narrative is produced against AFTS process-authority prompts and edited for publication. Figures and pathogen names are preserved verbatim from source data.
